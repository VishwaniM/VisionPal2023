Project Scope

Literature Survey

According to recent research, there has been a significant focus on developing assistance systems for visually impaired individuals, aiming to improve their quality of life and enable them to carry out daily activities more independently [1]. This has led to the development of new and improved techniques and wearable devices that provide precise and informative feedback [2]. Machine learning algorithms and image processing techniques have been explored to identify obstacles, vacant seats, crowd levels, road signs, pedestrian crossings, and traffic lights [3] [4] [5] [6] [7]. Future advancements in assistance systems are expected to be driven by the progress in computer vision, machine learning, and wearable technology, with a greater integration of other assistive technologies like screen readers and GPS devices [2].

Project Scope

Research Gap

The research gap in enhancing safe pedestrian travel for visually impaired individuals lies in the integration and development of a comprehensive system that effectively addresses obstacle detection, seating identification, road sign interpretation, and pedestrian crossing location. Current efforts focus on specific elements, such as obstacle detection and basic sign recognition, but there's a lack of a unified, intuitive solution that holistically caters to the varied needs of the visually impaired in navigating public spaces. Further research is required to amalgamate these individual components into a cohesive, user-friendly system that offers seamless assistance for the diverse challenges encountered by visually impaired individuals in their daily travels.

Project Scope

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

The challenge at hand involves the development and assessment of a comprehensive visual assistant system that can significantly improve the independence, safety, and overall quality of life for visually impaired individuals during their outdoor activities. Currently, visually impaired individuals face significant challenges while navigating outdoor environments, which can limit their degree of independence and their safety. Existing solutions often have less comprehensive features, failing to address the various requirements of visually impaired individuals in outdoor settings. A smart visual assistant system that provides real-time assistance, accurate navigational support, and easy communication with the surrounding environment is required to overcome this gap. This system should integrate advanced technologies and user friendly design elements to assist visually impaired individuals and increase a higher quality of life, promoting greater confidence and security in their outdoor activities.

Product Demonstration - Solution

Solution

To address the challenges faced by visually impaired individuals, the proposed solution involves the development of a cutting-edge smartphone application, designed as a comprehensive visual assistant system for the visually impaired. This system will offer real-time assistance to users, offering a range of user-friendly features. By utilizing advanced technologies such as image recognition, and audio feedback to provide essential information about the surrounding environment, the system will enable users to receive prompt and accurate guidance, enhancing their navigational capabilities and independence during outdoor activities. The integration of this user-friendly interface aims to significantly enhance the independence and overall quality of life for visually impaired individuals, enabling them to navigate outdoor environments with greater confidence and safety.

Project Scope

Research Objective

Develop a Smartphone-based Application That Will Assist Visually Impaired Individuals.

The main objective of this work is to ensure that the life of visually impaired individuals is made easier and more comfortable. Also, this work will ensure visually impaired people are more independent. Further, this work would ensure the safety of these individuals as well.

Obstacle Detection for Safe Pedestrian Travel.

The system utilizes a camera to detect obstacles within a designated perimeter, accurately identifying whether the detected obstacles are human beings or not. It will then generate a clear and precise vocal output to convey this information to the user. The development of this feature will prioritize user-friendliness, ensuring that the interface and overall functionality of the system are intuitive and easily accessible to visually impaired individuals.

Identify Vacant Seating and Empty Spaces that are Available in Public Areas and Public Transportation.

The visual assistant system aids visually impaired users in identifying unoccupied seats, providing specific guidance on the distance and precise location of the available seating. Moreover, the system will facilitate the selection of suitable seating arrangements by distinguishing between seating areas designated for males and females. It will also actively evaluate the extent of overcrowding in public spaces, empowering users to make informed decisions based on the current level of crowd density and occupancy.

Identify Road Signs and Road Text Signs.

The system assists visually impaired users in accurately and independently identifying road signs within a specified distance, enabling them to interpret and comprehend the information conveyed by these signs effectively. Additionally, it will support users in recognizing and understanding text signs, ensuring they can access essential textual information displayed in various contexts. Furthermore, the system will aid users in identifying specific signs, providing contextual information and guidance as needed. It will offer comprehensive voice assistance, delivering clear and detailed verbal instructions to enhance the user's understanding of the signs and their surroundings.

Identify the Color Light and Pedestrians Crossing for Crossing the Road.

The system is capable of identifying the location of pedestrian crossings equipped with traffic lights, ensuring the safe navigation of visually impaired users. It will provide clear and concise output, guiding users to the precise location of the crossing and enabling them to locate the appropriate traffic signals for a secure road-crossing experience.

Project Scope

Methodology

The proposed system "VisionPal" involves four key components.

1. Obstacle Detection for Safe Pedestrian Travel : This component will employ advanced camera-based technology to identify and detect obstacles in the environment, ensuring the safety of visually impaired pedestrians. The system will use image processing algorithms and depth perception techniques to accurately assess the distance and nature of obstacles, providing timely alerts or guidance to avoid potential danger.

2. Identify Vacant Seating and Empty Spaces that are Available in Public Areas and Public Transportation : The system will utilize a combination of visual recognition to identify unoccupied seating, vacant spaces and crowd level within the user's vicinity. It will employ sophisticated image analysis algorithms to detect and categorize different seating arrangements, providing real-time audio output to guide visually impaired users towards available seating options.

3. Identifying Road Signs and Text Signs : This component will leverage image recognition and text detection algorithms to identify and interpret road signs. The system will use machine learning models trained on a comprehensive dataset of road signs and signals to accurately classify and interpret visual information, providing audio-based descriptions and guidance to the user.

4. Traffic Lights and Pedestrian Crossing Assistance : The system will integrate location-based services and real-time traffic monitoring to assist visually impaired pedestrians in safely navigating pedestrian crossings, as well as recognize different color lights at pedestrian crossings. It will provide audio output and real-time feedback about the status of traffic lights and pedestrian crossing signals, enabling users to make informed decisions and safely crossroads. The system will also incorporate user-friendly interfaces and intuitive voice-based instructions to enhance the overall pedestrian crossing experience for visually impaired individuals.
